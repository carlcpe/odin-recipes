Filipino Pork Sisig Recipe

Recpie from Panlasang Pinoy

Description

Pork Sisig is a quintessential Filipino dish known for its bold flavors and crispy texture. Originating from the culinary capital of the Philippines, Pampanga, this dish features chopped pork, typically from the face, belly, and sometimes liver, that is boiled, grilled, and then sautéed with garlic, onions, and chilies. The addition of soy sauce, vinegar, and calamansi juice gives it a savory, tangy profile, while the optional inclusion of mayonnaise and a raw egg stirred in at the end adds a rich, creamy dimension.

Traditionally served on a sizzling plate, Pork Sisig is a favorite among Filipinos for its perfect balance of textures and flavors—crispy, tender, savory, and spicy. Often enjoyed as a main dish with rice or as a popular pulutan (appetizer) to accompany drinks, Sisig has become a beloved staple in Filipino cuisine, celebrated for its deliciously unique and hearty appeal.

Ingredients

    • 1 lb pig ears
    • 1 lb pig snout
    • 1 lb pork belly
    • 2 pieces onions, minced
    • 3 pieces bay leaves
    • 2 teaspoons salt
    • 4 thumbs ginger, crushed
    • 1 quart water

    Sisig Dressing:

    • ½ cup Lady’s Choice Mayonnaise
    • 2 tablespoons sukang iloko (see note 1)
    • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • ¼ cup liver spread (see note 2)
    • 2 limes (see note 3)
    • 1 tablespoon Knorr Liquid Seasoning

Steps

  1. Combine pig ears, snout, pork belly, ginger, bay leaves, 2 teaspoons salt, and 1 quart of water in a cooking pot. Boil for 1 hour. Remove from the pot and drain the water.
  2. Heat-up your grill. Start grilling the pig parts for around 5 minutes per side. Remove from the grill afterwards and let it cool down.
  3. Make the dressing by combining all the dressing ingredients. Mix well.
  4. Chop the grilled pig parts into small pieces. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped pork with onion and the dressing mixture. Toss until all the ingredients are well blended.
  5. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve with your favorite drink. Share and enjoy!
